How do we function?

If you’re new, don’t worry. Getting involved is simple.

First things first, send us an email. We want to get to know you. What’s your name and major? What year are you in school? Which publications are you most interested in, and which nights are you available to work?

We’ll take your information and schedule you to work for the publication(s) best suited for you. Around the lab, you’ll be known as a Multi-product designer. (Oh, and don’t worry if you’re new to the software or have never designed for a publication. We have an amazing group of editors who love teaching young designers.)

As you design on a regular basis, your portfolio will grow. You’ll become a better, more skilled designer, and your editors will take notice. Once a month, our editor team will hold portfolio reviews. During these review sessions, you’ll be able to show off your work to the editor team and art director who will decide if you’re ready for a Specialized Designer position.

The perks of a Specialized Designer position include more scheduled work nights (for more portfolio clips) and consideration from the editor team for special projects.

Want to keep moving up the ladder? Each semester we hold interviews for six Lead Designer positions. Three Lead Designers are responsible for feature spreads in Ball Bearings Magazines as well as one interactive tablet design for Ball State Weekly each week. The other three Lead Designers are responsible for two nights of work at the Ball State Daily News, including one A1 design.

Each designer reports to an editor. The Unified Media Design Lab has strong team of four editors and two assistant editors, each of whom reports to the art director. (Interviews for these positions occur each semester.)

If you have more questions about how we operate, please feel free to email us. We’re looking forward to working with you.
